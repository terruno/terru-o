BODEGAS SANCHA

The winery, located at the entrance to Baños de Río Tobía and surrounded by vineyards, evokes a small Burgundian château and represents the realisation of Juan Carlos Sancha’s dream: to craft his own wines that celebrate the region’s native grape, Garnacha, while reviving minority varieties at risk of extinction, such as Maturana Tinta, Maturana Blanca, and Monastel. He also works with Tempranillo Blanco, an indigenous Rioja variety.

The winery owns 24 hectares of vineyards, all planted in Baños de Río Tobía, at the heart of the Najerilla Valley—an extreme, frontier area within the DOCa. The entire vineyard is divided into very small plots with high planting density (5,000 vines per hectare, compared to the usual 3,000), on poor, shallow, calcareous soils. These conditions force the vines to limit their vigour and yield, resulting in wines that are intensely concentrated yet remarkably fresh—high in quality and bursting with character. These are honest wines, striving to express the terroir: that magical interplay of the soil in which they grow and the microclimate that shapes them.
